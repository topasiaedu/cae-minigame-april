# Copywriting Guide

## The 4 Core Principles

Every piece of text in this app must pass these four filters, in this order:

### 1. Mirror over Label
**Do not** tell the user who they are. Show them their own behaviour and let them recognise themselves.

| ❌ Label | ✓ Mirror |
|---|---|
| "You are an impulsive decision-maker." | "You start before everything is clear." |
| "You are risk-averse." | "You prefer to wait until the picture is clearer." |

### 2. Reflection over Explanation
Ask a question. Do not explain the answer. The silence between the question and the speaker's return is the most powerful moment.

| ❌ Explanation | ✓ Reflection |
|---|---|
| "This pattern means you may miss opportunities." | "Over time, does this quiet hesitation echo in other big choices?" |

### 3. Tension over Fluff
Include a "hidden cost" sentence in every result. The user should feel slightly uncomfortable — not insulted, but gently confronted.

| ❌ Fluff | ✓ Tension |
|---|---|
| "Your analytical style is a strength." | "But over time, the habit of calculating can quietly kill things before they even have a chance to begin." |

### 4. Open Loop over Closure
**Do not resolve the tension.** The final line of the game is a question, not an answer. The speaker closes the loop when they return. If the game provides closure, the webinar loses its moment.

---

## Reading Level Standard

All copy must be readable at an **8th-grade reading level** (Flesch-Kincaid Grade 8 or lower). This ensures:
- Non-native English speakers can follow along
- Older participants (55+) don't have to slow down to parse jargon
- The emotional hit lands faster

### Rules
- Maximum 20 words per sentence in the body of questions.
- Zero multi-syllable corporate jargon: no "optionality," "ROI," "synergy," "leverage."
- Replace abstract adjectives with human ones: not "analytical" but "you count the numbers before moving."

---

## Structure of a Good Question

```
Setup (bold sentence): Sets the scene. A business scenario.
Context (regular weight): Frames the stakes and the conflict. Ends with "You usually:" or "You decide to:".
Options (A/B/C/D): Each is a natural behavioural response, not a right/wrong answer.
```

**Setup and Context must be read as a single continuous sentence.** The setup is the "if" and the context is the "then what." Never write a setup that can be understood without the context.

---

## Structure of a Good Reflection Trigger

One paragraph. Maximum 3 sentences. Pattern:
1. Name the behaviour without judging it ("Some people naturally move rapidly...")
2. Describe it as a pattern over time ("Over time, does this...")
3. End with a question, not a statement

---

## Structure of a Good Result

### Line 1 — The Observation (muted)
State what they do, plainly and neutrally. No judgment.
> *"You start before everything is clear."*

### Line 2 — The Benefit (muted)
Acknowledge what works about this pattern. Validate them briefly.
> *"This keeps you in motion when others are still hesitating."*

### Line 3 — The Hidden Cost (highlighted gold box, italic)
The tension. The blind spot. One sentence only. The user should feel a quiet recognition, not shame.
> *"But over time, it becomes hard to tell the difference between moving with purpose — and simply staying busy."*

---

## The Final Quote (Section 04 — Bottom of Result)

This is the last thing the user reads before the speaker returns:

> *"What determines your path is rarely a single, brilliant choice."*
>
> *It is the quiet, automatic habit you fall back on every time you simply don't know what to do.*

**Rules for this quote:**
- Never changes regardless of dimension — it is universal.
- Must NOT answer anything. It must open a question.
- The speaker's return is what answers it.

---

## Things to Never Write

- Encouraging filler: "Great job!", "Well done!", "Interesting!"
- Explicit labels: "You are an Introvert / Thinker / Leader"
- Resolved tension: "Luckily, you can change this by..."
- Corporate vocabulary: strategy, leverage, synergize, optimize, ROI
- Generic inspirational quotes (the final quote is the only exception — and it deliberately does not inspire, it confronts)

---

## Tone Reference

Think of the tone as a **wise older mentor** who knows you well and is asking the question they've been waiting to ask. They are not judging. They are not praising. They are holding up a mirror.

The tone is: **calm, precise, slightly uncomfortable, deeply human.**
