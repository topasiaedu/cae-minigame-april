Result Page Structure & Content Logic
Purpose of the Result Page
The Result Page is designed to help participants:
* Recognise a familiar decision pattern
* Reflect on how this pattern shows up in real situations
* Develop a subtle awareness of its longer-term influence
It is not intended to:
* Provide a full psychological explanation
* Label personality traits
* Deliver a completed “assessment result”
Instead, the page creates an open reflective state before the live session.
________________

Header
Participant Name

Purpose:
Frame the experience as recognition, not evaluation.
________________


1. A closer look
Pattern chips provide a quick behavioural impression of the participant’s decision tendency.
They function as:
* Emotional recognition cues
* Not diagnostic labels
* Not conceptual frameworks
Final chip set:
* Early movement
* Adjust as you go
* Open options
* Before full clarity
* Short cycles
* Stay in motion
Design intention:
* One primary chip (visual anchor)
* Two supporting chips
* Three background chips
* Slight asymmetry to suggest an emerging pattern
________________


2. How you tend to respond
Slider signals visualise behavioural tendencies across different decision contexts.
Purpose:
* Show pattern through variation
* Reduce reliance on abstract explanation
* Enable intuitive self-recognition
This section acts as:
Evidence before interpretation
________________


3. What this pattern may mean
This section introduces a gentle interpretation of the behavioural pattern.
Tone requirements:
* Exploratory, not conclusive
* Reflective, not instructional
* Observational, not evaluative
Content goal:
* Connect behaviour → underlying tendency
* Introduce subtle tension (awareness gap)
* Avoid final psychological closure
This prepares participants for deeper reflection.
________________


Page 2 
Looking at the bigger picture
This section expands the perceived importance of repeated behavioural patterns.
Purpose:
* Shift from moment → trajectory
* Introduce temporal depth
* Create psychological pause
Tone:
* Quiet
* Non-directive
* Existential rather than analytical
Design intention:
* Increased whitespace
* Slower reading rhythm
* Reduced visual density
This supports transition into the live facilitation experience.
________________


Experience Flow (Psychological Sequence)
The intended internal journey is:
1. Pattern glimpse (chips)
2. Pattern confirmation (signals)
3. Pattern interpretation (insight)
4. Pattern significance (reflection)
End state:
Participants feel:
* Recognised
* Slightly unsettled
* Curious to understand more
Not:
* Diagnosed
* Concluded
* Fully explained
________________


Tone Principles for the Result Page
The page should feel:
* Reflective, not analytical
* Human, not technical
* Suggestive, not definitive
* Calm, not instructional
It should avoid resembling:
* Personality test reports
* Coaching worksheets
* Learning modules
* Data dashboards
Instead, it should resemble:
A quiet moment of self-realisation.




Result Reflection Text
从刚才的选择来看，
你在关键节点时，似乎会回到一种比较熟悉的处理方式。
这种方式可能让事情更容易开始，
也可能在不知不觉中影响你走向的方向。
很多人后来才发现，
不是某一次决定改变了人生，
而是长期重复的那种方式。
